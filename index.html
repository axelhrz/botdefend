How it works?

1.BotDefend Javascript tag collects browser signals.

Our tag is cached globally by AWS CloudFront and runs in 5ms or less.Dosen't slow down the page & users will never notice

2.AWS WAF process your traffic and logs key browser signals.

If you don't already use AWS CloudFront, you will need to set up & re-route your traffic. sensitive fields can be omitted from logs.

3.BotDefend reads logs & detect bad bots.

Our advanced algorithms can detect minute amounts of bad bots out of millions of human requests. On-premise solution not available.

4.Bad Bots are blocked with API calls to your WAF.

BotDefend dashboard allows you to configure custom rules based on request fields. One-click support for good bots and cummon third party integrations.

Comparison with existing solutions

Other solutions (CloudeFlare, Akamai, Imperva, Reblaze, etc) proxy every request in realtime, which is expensive & adds latency. To do this, the require heavy infrastructure or DNS changes.
Botdefend just processes the logs, which significantly lowers the price and provides a similar level of denfense. BotDefend requires no DNS changes or proxying inrastructure.

Frequently Asked Questions

What about clouds other than AWS?

We are gathering interest in other clouds(eg. Azure, GCPI).Contact us to express your interest. If you are using physical on-promise servers, we recomand you route all your traffic through AWS CloudFront.

Can I whitelist a custom 3rd party?

Of course! You can whitelist requests by URL, domain, IP adress, or any HTTP Header Field in the Deshboard.

But if you process request logs, you will miss a few requests right?

For a few minutes, yes. However, we block by IPadress and it is very expensive & difficult for scrappers to obtain new IP adresses every few minutes. Once blocked a few times, most will give up.

What about robots.txt?

Robots.txt is equivalent to politely asking scrapers not to scrape.Many will just ignore it and scrape anyway. BotDefend enforces these rules and blocks misbehaving bots.
